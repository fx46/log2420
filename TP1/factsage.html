FACT-Format Output

Let us examine the results for the following molar reaction at 1 atm and 298.15 K: 
 Mg    +    3 Cu2O  +   2 Fe3C    =   ?

Complete equilibrium is assumed (i.e. kinetics are ignored). In Equilib-Web, the possible equilibrium products are an ideal gas phase, pure solids and pure liquids. In the Equilib module of the full package, charged species (aqueous and gaseous ions), non-ideal solution data and additional databases can also be included.

The FACT-Format prints the computed equilibrium composition of the gas phase followed by all possible product solids and liquids, ordered with respect to activity. Even if the gas phase is not stable (as in the current example), the output still yields equilibrium potentials of the gaseous species.

From the Gibbs phase rule, F = C - P +2, F is degrees of freedom, C is the number of components, and P is the number of phases. In Equilib-Web the product T and P are constrained (i.e. F = 2). In the present example there are 5 elements (Mg, Cu, O, Fe and C) and so from the phase rule there will be up to 5 stable product phases.

The equilibrium products - Cu, Fe, C, MgO and Fe3O4 - satisfy the mass balance. The gas phase is treated as ideal ('gas_ideal'). The results show the sum of the gas partial pressures is 0.21821e-19 atm. Since the total pressure is 1 atm, the gas phase is not stable.

In the Equilib module of the full package the gas can be treated as real using the virial equation of state and gas coefficient data, and the product total pressure (or total volume) fixed at values other than unity. Compressibilities of the condensed phases also may be included in the computation.
